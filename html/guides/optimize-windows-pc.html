Step 4: Enhance Performance with Hellzerg's Optimizer

Warning: This tool hasn't been updated in over a year. Use at your own risk:

Download page for Optimizer

Keep in mind: For certain settings you enable or disable, a restart may be required to apply the changes, which is found in the top right corner of the interface.

General Optimizer Settings:

These control system-wide optimizations like privacy, customization, and performance.

Windows-Specific Optimizer Settings:

These tailor optimizations to Windows 10/11 features, including privacy and security.

Enable or disable features according to your preferences.

  1. Explore the 'General' and 'Windows (Version)' settings for a comprehensive overview.
  2. Click on each option's text for a detailed description before applying changes.
  3. Personalize your experience by adjusting these settings. We've provided recommendations, but feel free to customize based on your needs.

1. 'General':

  • Superfetch
  • Recommended for HDD users; otherwise disable it. Limited RAM may slow down your HDD system.
  • SMBv1 & SMBv2 Protocol
  • Recommended if sharing files on legacy hardware or using legacy printers.
  • Telemetry & Telemetry Tasks
  • Not recommended due to privacy concerns.

2. 'Windows (Version)':

  • Sensor Services
  • Recommended for laptops only. However, if the notebook has location services disabled, no touchscreen, and both auto-rotation and adaptive brightness turned off, you can safely disable the sensor services.
  • Virtualization-Based Security
  • Recommended to turn off Virtualization-Based Security if you don't use virtualization and don't care about memory integrity.
  • Telemetry Services
  • Again, not recommended due to privacy concerns.

Step 10: Advanced Optimization (BACKUP FIRST)

Backup Your Registry

Press the Windows key and type "reg edit" (short for Registry Editor).

Optimize with Registry Editor (reg edit)

Navigate to:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Control\PriorityControl

Enter one of the following values:

  • Gaming:
  • Hex: 14, 15, 16, 18 | Dec: 20, 21, 22, 24
  • Other:
  • Hex: 24, 25, 26, 2a, or 2 (default) | Dec: 36, 37, 38, 42, or 2

For further Registry Editor optimization, refer to: How to Change Split Threshold for svchost.exe in Windows 10.
